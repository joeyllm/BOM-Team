# 💬 Discord: The BOM Nowcasting Communication Hub

While GitHub is where our code lives, **Discord is where our team lives.** This server is the official headquarters for the BOM Nowcasting project. It is mandatory that you join, stay active, and use it as your primary tool for project communication.

---

## 📅 Internal Syncs & Meetings

We do not rely on slow, isolated email chains to build this project. 

* **Daily Communication:** If you are stuck on a pipeline, need clarification on a dataset, or want to share a breakthrough, post it in the relevant text channel. 
* **Team Meetings:** We will be using the Discord Voice/Video channels for our regular team meetings and syncs. Please ensure your microphone and headset are configured and ready to go before meeting times.

---

## 🌍 The Frontline for External Contributors

We are not building BOM Nowcasting in a vacuum. As the year progresses and the project gains momentum, **other developers and open-source contributors will be reaching out to join the effort.**

This is the most important reason we use Discord:
* **You are the first point of contact.** When external contributors join the server to ask how they can help or to understand the project architecture, they will be talking to *you*.
* **Build the Community:** By being active, answering questions, and discussing your pipelines openly, you help create a welcoming and highly competent environment for new talent to join.

---

## ✅ Etiquette & Expectations

Because this is a public-facing hub where external contributors will interact with us, please maintain a professional and collaborative standard:

* **Check in regularly:** Treat this like your digital office. You don't need to be online 24/7, but you should be checking for pings and updates every time you sit down to work on the project.
* **Keep it organized:** Use the specific channels (e.g., `#data-cleaning`, `#gpu-pipelines`) for their intended purposes so information is easy to find.
* **Be responsive:** If someone (especially an external contributor) asks a question in your domain, jump in and help them out!

---

**See you in the server!** 🚀
